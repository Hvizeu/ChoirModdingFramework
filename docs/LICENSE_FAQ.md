# License FAQ

This is a plain-language summary of common situations. It is not a replacement
for the [Choir Modding Framework Source-Available Contribution License
1.1](../LICENSE). The LICENSE text controls if this summary ever differs from it.

## Is Choir open source?

No. Its source is publicly visible, but Choir uses a custom source-available
license. GitHub may display the license as `Other`; that is expected.

## Can I download and use Choir?

Yes. You may download and run an official Choir release. Songs of Syx and any
consumer mods are installed separately.

## Can my mod use Choir?

Yes. Your original mod may compile against `choir.api` and declare Choir as a
separate dependency. Do not copy, shade, rename, embed, or redistribute Choir
classes or Choir's runtime JAR inside your mod.

## Can I fork the repository and send changes?

Yes. You may create a GitHub fork to develop, demonstrate, or submit proposed
contributions to the official project. Pull requests and focused patches are
welcome.

## Will contributors receive credit?

Yes. Accepted contributors are credited through Git history, release notes,
contributor records, or another reasonable project record.

## Can I publish my fork or my own Choir build?

Not without prior written authorization. A contribution fork is not an independent
distribution channel, and it may not publish releases or present itself as an
official Choir project.

## Can I include Choir in a mod pack, installer, mirror, or bundle?

Not without prior written authorization. Link users to an official Choir release
or official Workshop item instead.

## Why can Maven Central distribute `choir-api`?

The license gives Maven Central and its ordinary delivery infrastructure a narrow
exception to host and deliver the unmodified API artifacts officially published
by the copyright holder. That exception does not authorize anyone else to publish
Choir releases, modified builds, or look-alike coordinates.

## Can I claim Choir or an altered build as my own work?

No. Copyright, authorship, license, contributor, and third-party notices must be
preserved. Modified versions may not be represented as official or use the Choir
identity to imply endorsement.

## How do I request redistribution permission?

Contact Henrique Vizeu through an official Choir project page and describe exactly
what you want to distribute, where, in what form, and whether it is modified.
